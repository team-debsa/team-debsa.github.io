About This Tool

Introduction

Does taking notes in class lead to better grades? While note-taking is often seen as a key academic skill, it may not be the strongest predictor of success. This tool explores the relationship between note-taking frequency, class attendance, reading habits, and weekly study hours to uncover which factors truly drive higher GPAs.

Dataset

The data is from a study titled Student Performance Classification Using Artificial Intelligence Techniques, and includes information on student demographics, study habits, and GPA.

How to Interact

Use the following features to explore the tool

  • Filters: Select specific student groups based on age, gender, scholarship, etc. The visualization updates accordingly.
  • Sankey Diagram: Hover over links to see the number of students following that specific path and their percentage.
  • Key Insights: The "Key Insights" panel dynamically updates with significant findings based on your filters.
  • Study Habit Patterns: Display detailed connections between each study pattern on the current filters. Use this information to further explore the visual using filters.

Key Takeaways from the Data

While note-taking is beneficial, it is not the sole driver of academic success. Instead, a combination of factors—including class attendance, reading frequency, and weekly study hours—plays a significant role in shaping student performance.

Study Patterns of High-Performing Students (GPA 3.50+):

  • 92% consistently attend class—reinforcing the importance of engagement.
  • 76% maintain a regular reading habit, showing that exposure to diverse materials enhances learning.
  • 72% take notes frequently, but this alone doesn’t guarantee top performance.
  • 60% dedicate sufficient weekly hours to studying, emphasizing that quality study time matters more than sheer quantity.





    • What Does This Mean For Students

    Prioritize active engagement—attend classes, read consistently, and balance study techniques. The visualization challenges the assumption that note-taking alone ensures success. By leveraging the insights, students can make informed choices to optimize study strategies effectively.
